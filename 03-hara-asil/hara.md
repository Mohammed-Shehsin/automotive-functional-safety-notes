# HARA — Hazard Analysis and Risk Assessment

HARA is used to identify hazards, analyze risks, and derive safety goals.

## Course-Based HARA Steps

1. Item definition
2. Hazard identification
3. Hazard classification
4. Risk assessment

## Important Parameters

- Severity
- Exposure
- Controllability

## Engineering Example

For a braking system, a loss of braking at high speed has high severity and may require a high ASIL safety goal.

## How This Applies to Embedded Systems

Embedded engineers may contribute by understanding how software or hardware failures can create hazards.
