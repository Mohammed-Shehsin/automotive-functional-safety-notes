# Vector Functional Safety with ISO 26262 — Study Notes

These are personal study notes based on watched learning material titled Functional Safety with ISO 26262 – Principles and Practice.

## Topics Observed

- ISO 26262 as a complex and process-heavy standard
- ASIL-based risk classification
- HARA for deriving safety goals
- fail-safe vs fail-operational concepts
- risk reduction approaches
- connection between safety and cybersecurity
- legal liability and state-of-the-art engineering
- safety methods such as FMEA, FTA, FMEDA, dependent failure analysis, and ASIL decomposition

## Important Observation

Functional safety is not only about adding technical mechanisms. It also depends on process quality, documentation, traceability, verification, validation, and configuration management.

## Safety and Security

The material emphasized that cybersecurity can directly impact safety. For example, if a safety-related communication path is attacked, the system may create a hazardous event.

## Fail-Safe vs Fail-Operational

Fail-safe systems transition to a safe state after fault detection.

Fail-operational systems continue working in a degraded but safe mode. This is especially important for automated or continuous operation where immediate shutdown may not be acceptable.

## Risk Reduction

Risk reduction can include technical measures such as redundancy, diagnostics, self-tests, defensive programming, and modular architecture.

It also includes process measures such as safety management, configuration management, analysis methods, test methods, and safety case documentation.
